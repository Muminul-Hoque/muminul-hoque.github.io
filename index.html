About Me

I am Muhammed Muminul Hoque, an aspiring researcher with a deep interest in cybersecurity, cryptographic systems, and secure computing. I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Dhaka, where I built a strong foundation in theoretical and applied aspects of computing. I am currently preparing to pursue a PhD, aiming to contribute to the development of secure, privacy-preserving, and resilient technologies in the age of intelligent systems.

My academic focus lies at the intersection of cybersecurity, privacy, artificial intelligence, and system trust. I am especially motivated by the challenges posed by emerging technologies such as large language models, quantum computing, and cyber-physical systems. Through interdisciplinary research and rigorous inquiry, I seek to design and analyze systems that ensure security, enhance trust, and safeguard sensitive data in increasingly complex digital ecosystems.

Developing an Economical Risk Prediction Model for Acute Heart Failure in Dyspnea Patients (2025 - Ongoing)

  • Utilizing text mining techniques to integrate structured clinical data and unstructured text from electronic health records.
  • Applying algorithms like LightGBM for risk prediction and interpreting key features with SHAP for improved model interpretability.

Key Findings:

  • This research aims to create a predictive model to assess the risk of acute heart failure in patients with dyspnea, using a combination of structured clinical data and unstructured text data from EHRs.

  • The model is designed to be cost-effective and efficient, addressing real-world healthcare challenges with a focus on interpretability and accuracy.

Significance of Research: This project aims to improve the accuracy of acute heart failure risk prediction in dyspnea patients by integrating diverse data sources.
