Privacy Preserving and Secure Digital Identity Verification Mechanism (2025 - In Press)

  • Authors: Ekram Hoossain, Puja Rani Saha, Muhammed Muminul Hoque, Md. Saiful Islam, Jonayed Al-Faruk, Md. Alamgir Hossain

Key Findings:

  • The study presents a secure digital identity verification system that uses AES-256 encryption for protecting personal data and a two-factor OTP authentication to enhance user security.

  • The user-centric design enables smooth registration, secure key management, and reliable government verification, offering a balance between user convenience and security.
  • The system ensures low risk of identity theft and data breaches, making it highly suitable for digital service platforms, particularly in developing nations.

Significance of Research: The significance of this research lies in addressing the growing concerns over the security and privacy of digital identity verification systems. The research contributes to the advancement of digital identity systems by promoting a user-friendly yet highly secure verification process, which can be adopted by governments and companies to build trust and protect user privacy in digital transactions.



Journal Publication

Quantum-Resistant Blockchain (2025 - Submitted)

  • Enhanced Blockchain Security with ROUND5 (Lattice-based Cryptosystem)
  • Submitted to Q1 Journal
  • Authors: Md. Saiful Islam, Puja Rani Saha, Muhammed Muminul Hoque, AKM Bahalul Haque, Tushar Kanti Saha, Ekram Hossain

Key Findings:

  • ROUND5, a lattice-based cryptosystem, provides strong security against quantum attacks, making it a promising solution for securing blockchain systems in the future.
  • The study demonstrates that while traditional blockchain cryptography is vulnerable to quantum computing, ROUND5 offers a quantum-resistant alternative without significant performance costs.
  • The implementation of ROUND5 ensures scalability, security, and efficiency, positioning it as a feasible cryptographic solution for real-world blockchain applications.
  • The comparison of ROUND5 with other blockchain implementations shows that it is superior in terms of quantum resistance, offering a future-proof solution for blockchain security.

Significance of Research: This research is significant in the context of the rapidly evolving field of blockchain security, particularly in light of the potential threat posed by quantum computing. The paper explores the use of ROUND5, a lattice-based cryptographic algorithm, as a solution to strengthen blockchain systems against quantum attacks. As traditional cryptographic methods used in blockchain face the risk of becoming obsolete due to quantum advancements, this study provides a forward-thinking approach by introducing a quantum-resistant cryptographic solution that ensures the long-term security and integrity of blockchain technology. The integration of ROUND5 into blockchain protocols offers a promising route for enhancing data protection, maintaining privacy, and enabling secure transactions in the age of quantum computing. This research not only contributes to the security of blockchain systems but also ensures that these systems remain viable and secure as the field of quantum computing continues to develop.
